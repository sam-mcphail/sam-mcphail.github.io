Te Whare Tapa Whā

My wellbeing plan using Tā Mason Durie’s Te Whare Tapa Whā model

Using Tā Mason Durie’s Te Whare Tapa Whā model I am going to make a commitment to my physical, mental, spiritual and family well-being, as well as connecting more to nature.

Taha Tinana (Physical Wellbeing)

Like everyone, I feel better physically and mentally when I am active. To benefit my physical health, I am going to make a commitment to workout at the gym for an hour and a half at least 4 times a week.

Taha Whānau (Family Wellbeing)

I have been working from my parents house from time to time during foundations and plan to work there more often during bootcamp. To benefit my family wellbeing, I am going to make sure I am spending time with my parents whenever I am there.

Taha Hinengaro (Emotional and Mental Wellbeing)

I find my emotional and mental wellbeing is at its best when I am spending as much time as possible with friends. To help with this I am going to make sure that I spend a good amount of time with my flat mates every day and organise at least one activity to do with my friends every week.

Taha Wairua (Spiritual Wellbeing)

I have recently started doing breathing exercises before I start working. As I am not a very spiritual person, this is the closest thing I have to a spiritual activity. Therefore, I am going to do a five minute breathing exercise at least once a day.

Whenua (Interconnections to the land and environment)

With the weather warming up, I have started to spend more time outside. As the weeks go on and the weather gets better, I am going to spend at least half an hour a few times a week (weather permitting) to enjoy nature.
