Foundations Reflection

What are three new things you have learnt about yourself and your ego due to the core learning?

The first thing I learnt is how valuable it can be when I reflect on things afterwards. During the first week, I found that the reflections got in the way a bit and I didn’t really see the use in them. However, as the weeks have gone on, I have realised that when I reflect on a topic, my understanding of what I did, what I learned and how I did it increases. Secondly, I learnt that in the past I have shown a fixed mindset when it comes to learning. There were times at university when I didn’t understand concepts or ideas straight away and this led to me thinking that I would not be able to learn it whether I tried or not. This meant that some of the work I completed wasn’t up to the standard it should have been. Lastly, I learnt that practicing mindfulness can really help with focus.

What are the roles of values, empathy, and self-awareness in learning and programming?

Values are important in every part of life and are used in every decision you make. It’s no different when it comes to learning and programming. Values give you a reason to learn and they can help you out when you are stuck. Showing empathy comes into play when working with other people. Working in a group only works well when everyone is aware of how their actions are impacting others. Self-awareness is a lot like empathy in the fact that it comes into play when working with others. Being self-aware in a group basically comes down to being able to look at yourself from another person's perspective and see the impact of your actions.

What has surprised you the most about the core learning?

One big thing that surprised me about the core learning was just how much research has been put into every aspect of the human mind, learning and how they interact with each other. I think that this points to a change in the way scholars, educators and people in general are approaching learning. The two topics that come to mind when I think about this are neuroplasticity and growth mindset. The research into these two topics compliment each other and have led many people to the conclusion that just about anyone (especially young people) can learn just about anything using the right techniques and implementing the right mindset. Reading about this makes me believe that in the future there are going to be big changes in the way people learn at school, university and beyond.

What were the most challenging aspects of the core learning?

Definitely writing about myself. This is an aspect of core learning that I don’t think practice will help me get better at, or will take a lot of time at least. I think for the most part I am a private person when it comes to sharing my thoughts about myself or my emotions. It’s just something that makes me uncomfortable. However, I can see the value in doing the exercises that we have completed during the last five weeks. There is also the fact that putting yourself in uncomfortable positions can be good for learning and personal development, so the experience has been a positive one on the whole.

Why do you think we, a programming school, are spending so much time focusing on core learning in a web development Bootcamp course?

Employers aren’t just looking for people with coding experience. Sometimes programming experience isn’t even a requirement. The main thing they are looking for is a well-rounded person who can work well in a team and learn on the job. Every company will have a different set of tools they use to work. These can be learnt on the job. Something that is harder to learn is good core, human skills. The skills I have worked on over the last few weeks will not just make me a well-rounded programmer, but hopefully a more well-rounded person as well.

Does the time you spent studying core learning here feel like a waste of time? Should you have just used that time to practice programming instead?

It hasn’t been a waste of time for me personally. A lot of this has been new for me, so learning about it made me think about a lot of things (learning particularly) in a very different way. I will definitely utilise the skills I have learnt.
