CSS Inline vs Inline Blocks

Introduction

'display: inline' and 'display: inline-block' are two different ways to display boxes side by side in CSS. The main difference between the two is ‘inline’ doesn’t respect the top and bottom margins and paddings, but ‘inline-block’ does.

Example

In the examples below, you can see that when using inline, all of the boxes are side my side, however the top and bottom margins and paddings are uniform. In comparison, when using inline-block, all the boxes are side by side, but this time the top and bottom margins and paddings are respected and can be seen.
