Identity, Values and Strengths

These are my thoughts on my identity, values and sterngths.

Explain a situation where you have made an ethical decision. Discuss how you weighed up the values involved in that decision, the decision you made, and how you reflect on the decision now.

I think that in a way an ethical decision I made was to apply for Dev Academy. Having finished university at the end of last year, I wasn’t sure what I wanted to do with myself. I started working in warehousing after I left uni, but I felt that I needed to push myself and try something that really interested me. When the opportunity arose to apply for Dev Academy I thought it was the right thing to do for me. I felt like I owed it to myself to go for it and give it my best shot. I value self respect and what comes with that is putting myself in positions where I get the best out of myself and positions that will be beneficial for me in the long run.

Describe how your culture and the people around you have influenced your values and identity.

My culture and the people around me have caused me to value working with others highly. Socialising and spending time with the people I am close with is important to me and a big part of who I am. In the relationships I have, I value mutual respect. When working with new people I put effort into earning respect, but also begin the relationship by showing respect.

Identify your strengths and how they will support you during your learning journey.

A key strength of mine is problem solving. Throughout my life and in my studies especially, I have found that when a solution to a problem is not immediately clear, I am able to figure out a way to solve it. Another strength is my ability to always complete the task at hand. Whether working on a small task or a large scale project, I always make sure it gets finished and is done to a high standard.

Evaluate your limitations in terms of your learning and career development. How might these affect your learning journey?

When I am working, I have a tendency to procrastinate and mismanage my time. This often leads to needing to complete the majority of a task right at the end. Although I always complete my work to a standard I am happy with, it can cause a lot of stress when I don’t manage my time properly. I find that when working with others this is not as much of a problem because I have people relying on me, so I will have to focus on this when I am doing solo work.

Share an example of when you were trying to work productively with others, but there was resistance or tension. Discuss strategies you tried at the time, how effective they were, and your reflections on what other strategies you would try now and why.

During my time at university, I worked on many group projects. For the most part everyone would pull their weight and the team would be able to produce high quality work. There were a couple of instances where I worked in groups with one or more people who didn’t share the same drive to complete the task as the rest of the group. In one of these instances we had one member who only started communicating with the rest of us right before the due date. This caused a lot of stress for the group as we assumed their work would have been almost completed by this time, but they hadn’t even started it. This meant that the whole group had to pull together to complete this person's tasks. Looking back on this experience, I think that we probably all could have done more to communicate effectively as a team. I have learned that it is easier for everyone to rely on each other when there is good communication.
